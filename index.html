Featured Projects

Greetings! Let me tell you something. A huge number of devices are connected in computer networks. For example, the device from which you are reading this site. What can a network equipment failure lead to? It's probably not a big deal, you'll be without internet for a while. However, if there is a failure in the industrial network equipment, it can lead to serious losses.

Over the past few years, I have been involved in projects which improved the network diagnostics (and in other fascinating projects, too).

PROmanage NT®

Network management and monitoring software is used to prevent equipment failures and speed up the troubleshooting in the event of a failure.

As part of an agile team, I supported the software life cycle. As a Data Scientist with a small team of students, we Integrated an XGBoost ML Classifier for diagnostic PROFINET data into the app.

The classifier recognizes root causes of network failures to reduce the downtime of a network.

Data Analysis

Training a model may seem like an easy task, but there is a lot of science behind it. We've tested a number of approaches: anomaly detection, autoencoders, time-series analysis, cluster analysis and many others.

We developed, evaluated and optimized AI/ML algorithms for diagnostic PROFINET data. For the researches we collected, labeled and preprocessed data. The low-dimensional visualization of the data preprocessing result is depicted on the picture.

Genetic Algorithm in Electronics

During my studies I developed and evaluated the possibility of automated placement of electronic components on a printed circuit board using evolutionary algorithms. The genetic algorithm optimized two criteria: the total length of interconnections and heat dissipation. The open-source project was written in C# with Unity3D.
